Audiences

  • New Readers

    The Wikimedia movement seeks to increase readership in Asia, Africa, and Latin Americaâ€”places where new internet users are emerging but may not know or use Wikimedia sites. The New Readers research examines internet users and their context, assesses product development needs geared towards increasing engagement with Wikipedia for people in these contexts, and enhances our understanding of internet usage outside of high income countries.

  • New Editor Experiences

    New Editor Experiences is an investigation into users of mid-sized Wikis. This research identifies user characteristics, behaviors, and motivations using the Korean and Czech as representative examples of mid-sized Wikis. Then it examines how users interact with Wikipedia content and its interface. Insights from Czech and Korea are combined to create editor personas which are used to direct product development aimed at supporting users of mid-sized Wikis.

  • Machine Translation Meets Human Perception (Projected in 2019)

    The Machine Translation Meets Human Perception (MTMHP) Study develops a nuanced understanding of what acceptable quality means in different cultural contexts, for different content types, topical characteristics and reader motivations. We develop baseline framework for evaluating the quality of machine translated content across multiple languages starting with Arabic, Hindi, and Bahasa Indonesian. This research brings to light the unique features of machine translation and language interactions for each language of interest. It identifies which type of content is more successfully translated and pinpoints specific linguistic performance indicators that contribute to better and worse quality translation.

  • Editor Drop-Off (Projected in 2019)

    We have made great strides recently in keeping editors engaged on Wikipedia, but once they are retained, then what? This research identifies patterns of editor drop-off after a period of retention. We look at the editing "life span" of editors, their patterns of community interaction during that life span, the characteristics of editors engaged over longer periods, and the differences between editor drop-off patterns between different language wikis.
