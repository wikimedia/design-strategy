Powered by People

Understanding the needs, motivations and challenges of the people who power the free knowledge movement

Overview

Unlike many for-profit products, the twelve wikiprojects managed by the Wikimedia Foundation are as much about the people who read, write, support and monitor the creation of content as they are about the technologies that enable these activities. Our contributors work on over 800 projects worldwide, nearly 300 of which are different language versions of Wikipedia. This means our product teams need to understand how our readers access and utilize wiki content, what motivates and challenges the people who edit and moderate content, and how that varies across different languages, geographies and social contexts.

Studies

  1. Commons Administrator Interviews

    Oct 2023 Research to understand Commons Administrators workflows, the challenges they face in moderating content, and their ideas for reducing deletions.

  2. Campaign Event Discovery Survey

    Sep 2023 Survey on most common and most effective campaign event discoverability pathways.

  3. Commons Uploader Experience

    Jul 2023 A project to understand the user experience of uploading images to Commons, with the purpose of improving the upload process while reducing deletion requests...

  4. Journey Transitions

    May 2023 This project explores what and whether notable moments cause users to deepen and expand their use of and/or contributions to Wikipedia.

  5. Wikistories Early Adopters Research

    Jan 2023 A project about Wikistory adopters, both creators and readers, focusing on their motivations and experiences creating and sharing stories.

  6. Event Organizers: A Study in 4 African Countries

    March 2022 This exploratory research project focused on organizations in four african countries to learn about the particular challenges faced event organizers and the strategies they have developed to succeed in growing their communities.

  7. Content Moderation in Medium-Sized Wikimedia Projects

    March 2022 This project aims to fill knowledge gaps in our understanding of how editors curate and moderate content on Wikimedia projects outside the largest and most well-researched communities.

  8. Communications and Mentorship

    Dec 2021 Understanding the wiki-related communications ecosystem, both on and off Wiki, that embedded community members employ for their Wiki related work.

  9. Understanding Perspectives on Digital Education in African Contexts

    May 2021 Qualitative research examining the perspectives on the future of education from experts, students, teachers, and parents.

  10. How Do News Organizations Use Wikipedia?

    Jun 2020 A literature review that seeks to understand more about how and why media and news organizations use Wikipedia.

  11. Free Knowledge Movement Personas

    May 2020 This is a collection of all personas developed to date for desktop and mobile readers, new and veteran contributors, institutional curators and Movement organizers.

  12. Content Moderation Explained

    Jun 2020 An overview of how a global community of volunteer moderators determine which contributions to Wikipedia are accepted and rejected.

  13. CheckUser Workflow & Tools

    Apr 2020 CheckUser is a critical tool for moderators in anti-vandalism efforts on Wikipedia. This research surfaces the pain points in the CheckUser workflow and opportunities to improve the tool.

  14. Why Do People Edit Wikipedia?

    Feb 2020 This literature review examines the drivers of engagement and the motivators of sustained involvement in Wikipedia. It provides an overview of academic research on Wikipedia production and editor motivations, highlighting the themes and queries of scholarship in this area.

  15. WMF Remote Worker Experience

    Jan 2020 This is a lean, inward-facing research project that surfaced the challenges and highlights of working remotely for the Foundation and offered opportunities for the Foundation and non-remote staff to improve the work lives and for its globally distributed staff.

  16. New Page Patrol

    Sep 2019 Content integrity on Wikipedia relies on editors who patrol articles and respond to vandalism. This research brings to light the workflows of editors who patrol on Wikipedia(s) and the tools used in the course of their work.

  17. Movement Organizers

    Jun 2019 The Movement Organizer Study sought to understand the life cycle of contributors by documenting paths, practices, challenges, and risks faced by successful organizers throughout the Free Knowledge Movement.

  18. Contribution Workflow Taxonomy

    2018 This project details the workflow of contributors and assesses the level of difficulty they encounter with each step in the workflow in order to understand what contributes to editors' retention or burn-out.

  19. New Editor Experiences

    2017 This study sought to understand the experiences, needs and challenges of new editors on midsize Wikipedias with the goal of learning how best to support and retain them.

  20. New Readers

    2016 This study sought to understand potential Wikimedia readers in countries where access to the internet is quickly growing.

  21. Contribution Taxonomy Project

    2012 This project creates a taxonomy of the contributions made by Wikimedia communities, measure the contributions in that taxonomy, and elicit trends in volunteer contributions to the projects.
